Drones a 'game changer' for rescue teams

Drones have a growing role in the work of Scottish mountain rescue teams, says Search and Rescue Aerial Association (Scotland). Today there are 19 pilots and 21 drones across nine Scottish Mountain Rescue-member teams. There were none four years ago. Chairman Tom Nash of SARAA Scotland, said in a short number of years drone technology had developed from use in daytime only to having zoom-capable cameras, thermal imaging and floodlights for night-time missions.

Incandescent Light Bulbs to Be Phased Out in 2023

The end of an era is coming for incandescent light bulbs. The Department of Energy put two new rules on the books this week banning the production of these energy-sucking light sources in 2023, at which time manufacturers will be able to sell only energy-efficient light bulbs. The Energy Department said that once the rules take effect in 2023, consumers will collectively save close to $3 billion per year on utility bills. The planet will also benefit, the agency notes, as carbon emissions will be slashed by 222 million metric tons over the next 30 years. That's equal to the emissions produced by 28 million homes in a year.

Stolen fingerprints could spell the end of biometric security

The biggest known biometric data breach to date was reported recently when researchers managed to access a 23-gigabyte database of more than 27.8m records including fingerprint and facial recognition data. This breach highlights a major problem with biometric security systems that effectively use people's biological measurements as passwords. Unlike usernames and passwords, biometric data can't be changed if it is stolen.
